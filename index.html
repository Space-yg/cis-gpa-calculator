How to use the calculator?

Choose your grade and enter the grades for each subject.

What is GPA?

GPA stands for Grade Average Point. It is a form of way to evaluate grades. It is usually used on a 4.0 scaling point, meaning that the GPA is calculated out of 4.

What is CGPA?

CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Average Point. It is the average of all GPAs achieved. For example, CGPA can be calculated by getting the average between the first school term and the second school term GPAs. Another example is getting the average GPA from grade 9 through 12.

What is the difference between weighted and unweighted GPA?

Weighted GPA gives more grades to AP, honors, IB, and college classes. Unweighted GPA does not give any more grades to them. Weighted GPA gives 0.5 GPA more to honors classes and 1 GPA more to AP, IB, and college classes. Capital International Schools does not provide weighted GPA, but gives 10% more for AP classes.

How do you calculate unweighted GPA?

The calculation is actually simple.

  1. Get the grade percentage or letter of each class and use the Grade's Key to get each class's GPA.
  2. Multiply each GPA by the amount of credit of the class. Each class is usually 1 credit hour.
  3. Add all the GPAs of all the classes together.
  4. Divide the sum by the total of credit hours taken.
  5. Voila! You have your GPA!

How do you calculate weighted GPA?

Do the same calculation for as the unweighted GPA, but make sure that after step 1 you add 0.5 GPA to honors classes and 1 GPA to AP, IB, or college classes. Capital International Schools does not provide weighted GPA, but gives 10% more for AP classes.

What is AP?

AP stands for Advanced Placement. It is an series of exams made by the College Board that a student can choose from. They give a change for the student to take college-level work while they are still in school. They can sometimes be exchanged fro college credits. For more information, visit the AP website.
